Mammals

Mammals are warm-blooded animals that have hair or fur and produce milk for their young. Examples include lions, elephants, and whales.

Birds

Birds are feathered, winged, egg-laying vertebrates. They have beaks and lightweight skeletons. Examples include eagles, parrots, and penguins.

Reptiles

Reptiles are cold-blooded animals with scales. They lay eggs and breathe air. Examples include snakes, crocodiles, and turtles.

Amphibians

Amphibians live both in water and on land. They have moist skin and lay eggs in water. Examples include frogs, salamanders, and newts.
